## Airbnb Core Values & What They Evaluate

Airbnb's culture evaluation is built around their **core values**. Understanding these is essential — interviewers are specifically trained to assess for these.

### "Be a Host" — The Central Theme
At Airbnb, "Be a Host" means creating a sense of **belonging** for everyone — colleagues, customers, and communities. It's not just about hospitality; it's about making people feel welcomed, supported, and included.

**What they look for:**
- Empathy and care for others (teammates, customers, cross-functional partners)
- Going above and beyond to help someone — not just doing the minimum
- Creating inclusive environments where diverse perspectives are valued
- Being a "host" to your team — onboarding new members, sharing knowledge generously

### Core Evaluation Dimensions

| Dimension | What They Assess | Red Flags |
|---|---|---|
| **Belonging / Inclusion** | Do you create environments where everyone feels welcome? | Self-centered stories, no mention of team |
| **Cross-Functional Collaboration** | Can you work across teams, orgs, and cultures? | Only working in silos, "us vs them" language |
| **Ownership & Drive** | Do you take initiative and follow through? | Waiting for instructions, blaming others |
| **Empathy & Hospitality** | Do you genuinely care about the people around you? | Transactional relationships, zero-sum mindset |
| **Growth Mindset** | Do you learn from failures and help others grow? | Defensive about mistakes, no self-reflection |

## Questions by Theme

### Cross-Functional Collaboration (🔴 High Priority)

> JD: "collaborating with cross-functional and cross-geographical teams" — central to the role

1. **"Tell me about a time you worked with a team outside your own to deliver a project."**
   - Source: Training Knowledge (common Airbnb)
   - Prep Tip: Focus on how you bridged communication gaps, aligned on goals, and built trust. Mention specific teams (product, QA, ops, data)

2. **"Describe a situation where you had to convince another team to change their approach."**
   - Source: Training Knowledge
   - Prep Tip: Show influence without authority. Emphasize data/evidence-based persuasion, not pulling rank

3. **"Tell me about a time you collaborated with someone very different from you (different background, working style, or timezone)."**
   - Source: Training Knowledge (Airbnb values belonging + global teams)
   - Prep Tip: This directly maps to "belonging" — show cultural awareness and adaptability

4. **"How do you handle disagreements with cross-functional partners?"**
   - Source: Training Knowledge
   - Prep Tip: Show empathy first ("I sought to understand their perspective..."), then data-driven resolution

5. **"Give an example of driving a large-scale migration or adoption project across multiple teams."**
   - Source: Analysis Doc (JD requirement)
   - Prep Tip: JD specifically mentions this — prepare a strong story about coordinating a multi-team technical initiative

### Incident Response & Reliability (🔴 High Priority)

> JD: "leading and coordinating RCAs", "improve incident management platforms" — core role function

6. **"Tell me about a time you were involved in a production incident. How did you handle it?"**
   - Source: Training Knowledge
   - Prep Tip: Walk through detection → triage → mitigation → root cause → prevention. Show calm under pressure

7. **"Describe a time you identified and fixed a root cause that was causing recurring issues."**
   - Source: Analysis Doc (JD: "fix root causes from repeat issues")
   - Prep Tip: Show systematic thinking — not just fixing symptoms but building lasting solutions

8. **"How do you ensure reliability in the systems you build?"**
   - Source: Training Knowledge
   - Prep Tip: Mention monitoring, alerting, testing strategies, graceful degradation, circuit breakers — the JD focus areas

9. **"Tell me about a time something you built broke in production. What happened and what did you learn?"**
   - Source: Training Knowledge
   - Prep Tip: Own the mistake — Airbnb values growth mindset. Focus on what you learned and what you changed

10. **"How do you approach on-call responsibilities?"**
    - Source: Analysis Doc (resume gap — no explicit on-call experience)
    - Prep Tip: Even if limited on-call experience, discuss your approach to operational readiness and production support

### Technical Leadership (🔴 High Priority)

> JD: "Influencing architectural decisions", "drive large scale migration"

11. **"Tell me about a time you influenced an architectural decision that had significant impact."**
    - Source: Training Knowledge
    - Prep Tip: Show decision-making framework — trade-off analysis, not just "I decided X"

12. **"Describe a technical decision you made that others disagreed with. How did you handle it?"**
    - Source: Training Knowledge
    - Prep Tip: Show you listened, considered alternatives, used data, and could change your mind if warranted

13. **"Tell me about a system or service you designed from scratch. Walk me through the key decisions."**
    - Source: Glassdoor (project deep dive round)
    - Prep Tip: This is the "technical experience" round — prepare a detailed 10-15 minute walkthrough of a major project

14. **"How do you approach code reviews? What do you look for?"**
    - Source: Blind (code review round exists), Analysis Doc (JD mentions code reviews)
    - Prep Tip: Airbnb has a **dedicated code review round** — discuss readability, correctness, performance, test coverage, design patterns

15. **"Tell me about a time you challenged the status quo and followed through to completion."**
    - Source: Analysis Doc (JD: "challenge the status quo")
    - Prep Tip: Show initiative — you identified something suboptimal, proposed a change, and saw it through despite resistance

20. **"What are your goals?"**

26. **"Tell me about a time you improved a process or system that was inefficient."**
    - Source: Training Knowledge

27. **"How do you evaluate and adopt new technologies?"**
    - Source: Analysis Doc (JD: "evaluate new technologies including AI/ML")

28. **"Give us an example of a piece of work you have completed recently."**
    - Source: Glassdoor (live-fetched, Jul 2024 — London)
    - Prep Tip: Pick something impactful and recent — walk through problem, approach, result, and learnings

    - Source: Training Knowledge
    - Prep Tip: Show intentional career narrative — growth at each stage, increasing scope and impact

30. **"Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years?"**
    - Source: Training Knowledge

31. **"What's the most impactful project you've worked on?"**
    - Source: Training Knowledge

    - Source: Training Knowledge

## STAR Story Preparation Checklist

Prepare **8 well-crafted STAR stories** that can flex across multiple question themes:

| # | Story Theme | Maps To | Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Led cross-functional project (Pepstudy 6-microservice architecture) | Cross-functional, Technical Leadership | Team size, services built, performance gains |
| 2 | Established engineering standards (Swiss Re guidelines for 25-person team) | Mentoring, Operational Excellence, Technical Leadership | Adoption rate, defect reduction |
| 3 | Payment integration (Razorpay at Pepstudy) | Domain expertise, Technical Decision-Making | Transaction volume, success rate |
| 4 | Production debugging / incident response | Incident Response, Reliability | Time to resolution, prevention measures |
| 5 | Helping a colleague grow / mentoring | Belonging, Mentoring | Person's growth outcome |
| 6 | Challenging a technical decision and winning team over | Technical Leadership, Status Quo | Impact of the change |
| 7 | Building something from scratch with ambiguity | Ownership, Problem-Solving | Business impact |
| 8 | Cross-cultural / inclusion story | Belonging, "Be a Host" | Team dynamic improvement |

> **Key:** Each story should be 2-3 minutes long, have specific metrics, and naturally demonstrate Airbnb values without forcing it.
