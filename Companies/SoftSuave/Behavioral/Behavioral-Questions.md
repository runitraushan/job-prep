## 1. Cross-Functional Collaboration

> IT consulting = working with client stakeholders constantly. Soft Suave builds AI/ML solutions for multiple clients — they need someone who can translate business requirements into technical solutions.

1. "Tell me about a time you worked with a non-technical stakeholder to define requirements for an ML project."
2. "Describe a situation where you had to explain a complex ML concept to a product manager or business user."
3. "How have you collaborated with data engineers to design and implement data pipelines?"
4. "Tell me about a time when stakeholder requirements conflicted with technical feasibility. How did you handle it?"
5. "Describe a project where you worked across multiple teams (data, engineering, product). What was your role?"

**STAR Prep Tips:**
- Lead with the business problem, not the technology
- Show that you actively sought stakeholder input rather than building in isolation
- Emphasize translating between "business speak" and "ML speak"
- Use examples from cross-functional projects (AIOps at Wabtec, AP government project)

## 2. Problem Solving with ML

> Core of the role — translating business problems to ML solutions. Soft Suave wants to see you can independently identify where ML adds value and design appropriate solutions.

1. "Walk me through a business problem you solved using ML. How did you decide ML was the right approach?"
2. "Tell me about a time an ML model didn't perform as expected. How did you debug and improve it?"
3. "Describe a situation where you had to choose between multiple ML approaches. What factors influenced your decision?"
4. "Have you ever decided NOT to use ML for a problem? What was the alternative?"
5. "Tell me about the most impactful ML solution you've built. How did you measure its impact?"

**STAR Prep Tips:**
- Always quantify impact (MTTR reduced 40%, ticket volume reduced 20%, OCR accuracy 87%)
- Show systematic debugging process (data → features → model → hyperparameters → evaluation)
- Demonstrate that you consider simpler solutions before jumping to complex ML
- Frame answers around the business impact, not just technical achievement

## 3. Continuous Learning & Staying Updated

> Explicitly in JD: "Stay updated with the latest developments in AI/ML research and apply them to improve our systems." Critical signal for this role.

1. "How do you stay current with the rapidly evolving AI/ML field?"
2. "Tell me about a recent AI/ML technique or paper you learned about and applied in your work."
3. "Describe a time when you proactively adopted a new technology (e.g., LangChain, LangGraph) before it was widely used."
4. "How do you evaluate whether a new AI/ML tool or framework is worth adopting?"
5. "Tell me about a time you had to learn a completely new technology quickly for a project."

**STAR Prep Tips:**
- Be specific: name papers, frameworks, tools (not just "I read blogs")
- Show application, not just awareness — "I learned X and used it to improve Y by Z%"
- Reference GenAI journey: moving from traditional ML to RAG, LangChain, Agentic AI
- Mention specific learning habits: arxiv, conferences, hands-on experimentation
- MCP is your opportunity here — if you've learned it, discuss the learning journey

## 7. Data Quality & Integrity

> Critical for production ML. IT consulting clients often have messy data — handling it is a differentiator.

1. "Tell me about a time you discovered data quality issues that impacted model performance. How did you handle it?"
2. "How do you ensure data integrity in your ML pipelines?"
3. "Describe your approach to data validation before model training."
4. "Have you dealt with a situation where the training data was biased? What did you do?"
5. "How do you handle noisy or incomplete data in production ML systems?"

**STAR Prep Tips:**
- Show proactive data quality practices, not just reactive fixes
- Reference specific tools/techniques: data validation schemas, statistical tests, monitoring
- Connect to business impact: "Bad data led to X, so I implemented Y, which prevented Z"
- Mention data pipeline monitoring and alerting you've set up

## Prep Notes

- **Use STAR format** for every answer: Situation (1-2 sentences), Task, Action (bulk), Result (quantified)
- **Prepare 6-8 detailed stories** adaptable to different question types
- **Time per answer:** 3-4 minutes. Too short = lacks depth. Too long = loses interviewer.
- **IT consulting cultural notes:** They value client-facing skills, adaptability, and breadth over extreme depth in one area.
- **Key stories to prepare:**
  1. RAG chatbot project (GenAI, end-to-end, impact)
  2. BERT ticket classifier (ML, production, metrics)
  3. AIOps anomaly detection (cross-domain, technical depth)
  4. OCR + NLP pipeline (adaptability, new domain — government)
  5. LLM-assisted RCA (recent GenAI, problem solving)
  6. Cross-team collaboration story (stakeholder management)
  7. Learning a new technology quickly (LangChain/LangGraph adoption)
  8. Data quality challenge (debugging, production mindset)
